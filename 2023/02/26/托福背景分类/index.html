1.Sun

The Sun is the Solar System’s star and by far its most massive component. Its large mass (332,900 Earth masses), which comprises 99.86% of all the mass in the Solar System, produces temperatures and densities in its core high enough to sustain nuclear fusion of hydrogen into helium, making it a main sequence star. This releases an enormous amout of energy, mostly radiated into space as electromagnetic radiation peaking in visible light.

太阳是太阳系的恒星，也是迄今为止质量最大的组成部分。 它的大质量（332,900 个地球质量）占太阳系总质量的 99.86%，在其核心产生的温度和密度足以维持氢核聚变为氦，使其成为一颗主序星。 这释放出巨大的能量，大部分以可见光中达到峰值的电磁辐射形式辐射到太空中。

The Sun is a population I star; it has a higher abundance of elements heavier than hydrogen and helium than the older population II stars. Elements heavier than hydrogen and helium were formed in the cores of ancient and exploding stars, so the first generation of stars had to die before the Universe could be enriched with these atoms. The oldest stars contain few metals, whereas born later have more. This high metallicity is thought to have been crucial to the Sun’s development of a planetary system because the planets form from the accretion of “metals”.

太阳是I星群； 与年龄较大的星族 II 恒星相比，它拥有更多比氢和氦重的元素。 比氢和氦更重的元素是在古老的爆炸恒星的核心中形成的，因此第一代恒星必须在宇宙中充满这些原子之前死亡。 最古老的恒星含有很少的金属，而较晚诞生的则含有更多。 这种高金属性被认为对太阳行星系统的发展至关重要，因为行星是由“金属”的增生形成的。

2. Inner Solar System

The inner Solar System is the region comprising the terrestrial planets and the asteroid belt. Composed mainly of silicates and metals, the objects of the inner Solar System are relatively close to the Sun; the radius of this entire region is less than the distance between the orbits of Jupiter and Saturn.

内太阳系是由类地行星和小行星带组成的区域。 内太阳系天体主要由硅酸盐和金属组成，距离太阳较近； 整个区域的半径小于木星和土星轨道之间的距离。

The four terrestrial or inner planets have dense, rocky compositions, few or no moons, and no ring systems. They are composed largely of refractory minerals, such as the silicates—which form their crusts and mantles—and metals, such as iron and nickel, which form their cores. Three of the four inner planets (Venus, Earth and Mars) have atmospheres substantial enough to generate weather; all have impact craters and tectonic surface features, such as rift valleys and volcanoes.

这四颗类地行星或内行星具有致密的岩石成分，很少或没有卫星，也没有环系统。 它们主要由耐火矿物组成，例如形成地壳和地幔的硅酸盐，以及形成地核的铁和镍等金属。 四颗内行星中的三颗（金星、地球和火星）的大气足以产生天气； 所有这些都有撞击坑和构造表面特征，例如裂谷和火山。

Mercury

Mercury (0.4 AU from the Sun) is the closest planet to the Sun and on average, all seven other planets. The smallest planet in the Solar System, Mercury has no natural satellites. Besides impact craters, its only known geological features are lobed ridges or rupes that were probably produced by a period of contraction early in its history. Mercury’s very tenuous atmosphere consists of atoms blasted off its surface by the solar wind. Its relatively large iron core and thin mantle have not yet been adequately explained. Hypotheses include that its outer layers were stripped off by a giant impact, or that it was prevented from fully accreting by the young Sun’s energy.

水星（距离太阳 0.4 个天文单位）是离太阳最近的行星，平均来说，所有其他七颗行星。 水星是太阳系中最小的行星，没有天然卫星。 除了撞击坑外，它唯一已知的地质特征是可能由其历史早期的一段收缩期产生的叶状山脊或卢比。 水星非常稀薄的大气层由被太阳风吹离其表面的原子组成。 它相对较大的铁核和较薄的地幔尚未得到充分解释。 假设包括它的外层被巨大的撞击剥离，或者它被年轻太阳的能量阻止完全吸积。

Venus

Venus (0.7 AU from the Sun) is close in size to Earth and, like Earth, has a thick silicate mantle around an iron core, a substantial atomsphere, and evidence of internal geological activity. It is much drier than Earth, and its atmosphere is ninety times as dense. Venus has no natural satellites. It is the hottest planet, with surface temperatures over 400℃, most likely due to the amount of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere. No definitive evidence of current geological activity has been detected on Venus, but it has no magnetic field that would prevent depletion of its substantial atmosphere, which suggests that its atmosphere is being replenished by volcanic eruptions.

金星（距太阳 0.7 个天文单位）的大小与地球接近，并且与地球一样，在铁核周围有一层厚厚的硅酸盐地幔、一个坚固的大气层和内部地质活动的证据。 它比地球干燥得多，大气层的密度是地球的九十倍。 金星没有天然卫星。 它是最热的行星，表面温度超过 400 摄氏度，这很可能是由于大气中大量的温室气体所致。 没有在金星上发现当前地质活动的确切证据，但它没有磁场可以防止其大量大气层耗尽，这表明它的大气层正在通过火山喷发得到补充。

Earth

Earth (1 AU from the Sun) is the largest and densest of the inner planets, the only one known to have current geological activity, and the only place where life is known to exist. Its liquid hydrosphere is unique among the terrestrial planets, and it is the only planet where plate tectonics has been observed. Earth’s atmosphere is radically different from those of the other planets, having been altered by the presence of life to contain 21% free oxygen. It has one natural satellite, the Moon, the only large satellite of a terrestrial planet in the Solar System.

地球（距太阳 1 个天文单位）是内行星中最大、密度最大的行星，是已知唯一具有当前地质活动的行星，也是已知存在生命的唯一地方。 它的液态水圈在类地行星中是独一无二的，也是唯一观测到板块构造的行星。 地球的大气层与其他行星的大气层截然不同，它因生命的存在而发生改变，含有 21% 的游离氧。 它有一颗天然卫星，月球，是太阳系中唯一一颗类地行星的大型卫星。

Mars

Mars (1.5 AU from the Sun) is smaller than Earth and Venus. It has an atmosphere of mostly carbon dioxide with a surface pressure of 6.1 millibars (roughly 0.6% of that of Earth). Its surface, peppered with vast volcanoes, such as Olympus Mons, and rift valleys, such as Valles Marineris, shows geological activity that may have persisted until as recently as 2 million years ago. Its red colour comes from iron oxide (rust) in its soil. Mars has two tiny natural satellites (Deimos and Phobos) thought to be either captured asteroids, or ejected debris from a massive impact early in Mars’s history.

火星（距太阳 1.5 个天文单位）比地球和金星小。 它的大气层主要是二氧化碳，表面压力为 6.1 毫巴（大约是地球压力的 0.6%）。 它的表面散布着巨大的火山，如奥林匹斯山和裂谷，如水手谷，表明地质活动可能一直持续到最近的 200 万年前。 它的红色来自土壤中的氧化铁（铁锈）。 火星有两颗微小的天然卫星（Deimos 和 Phobos），它们被认为要么是被捕获的小行星，要么是火星早期历史上一次巨大撞击产生的碎片。

Asteroid belt

Asteroids except for the largest, Ceres, are classified as small Solar System bodies and are composed mainly of refractory rocky and metallic minerals, with some ice. They range from a few metres to hundreds of kilometres in size. Asteroids smaller than one meter are usually called meteoroids and micrometeoroids (grain-sized), depending on different, somewhat arbitrary definitions.

除了最大的谷神星外，小行星都被归类为太阳系小天体，主要由难熔岩石和金属矿物组成，还有一些冰。 它们的大小从几米到数百公里不等。 小于一米的小行星通常被称为流星体和微流星体（颗粒大小），这取决于不同的、有些武断的定义。

The asteroid belt occupies the orbit between Mars and Jupiter, between 2.3 and 3.3 AU from the Sun. It is thought to be remnants from the Solar System’s formation that failed to coalesce because of the gravitational interference of Jupiter. The asteroid belt contains tens of thousands, possibly millions, of objects over one kilometre in diameter. Despite this, the total mass of the asteroid belt is unlikely to be more than a thousandth of that of Earth. The asteroid belt is very sparsely populated; spacecraft routinely pass through without incident.

小行星带位于火星和木星之间的轨道上，距离太阳 2.3 到 3.3 个天文单位。 它被认为是太阳系形成的残余物，由于木星的引力干扰而未能合并。 小行星带包含数万个、可能数百万个直径超过一公里的物体。 尽管如此，小行星带的总质量不太可能超过地球的千分之一。 小行星带人烟稀少； 航天器经常平安无事地通过。

3. Outer Solar System

The outer region of the Solar System is home to the giant planets and their large moons. The centaurs and many short-period comets also orbit in this region. Due to their greater distance from the Sun, the solid objects in the outer Solar System contain a higher proportion of volatiles, such as water, ammonia, and methane than those of the inner Solar System because the lower temperatures allow these compounds to remain solid.

太阳系的外部区域是巨行星及其大卫星的家园。 半人马和许多短周期彗星也在这个区域运行。 由于与太阳的距离较远，外太阳系中的固体物体比内太阳系的固体物体含有更高比例的挥发物，例如水、氨和甲烷，因为较低的温度允许这些化合物保持固态。

The four outer planets, or giant planets (sometimes called Jovian planets), collectively make up 99% of the mass known to orbit the Sun. Jupiter and Saturn are together more than 400 times the mass of Earth and consist overwhelmingly of the gases hydrogen and helium, hence their designation as gas giants. Uranus and Neptune are far less massive—less than 20 Earth masses each—and are composed primarily of ices. For these reasons, some astronomers suggest they belong in their own category, ice giants. All four giant planets have rings, although only Saturn’s ring system is easily observed from Earth. The term superior planet designates planets outside Earth’s orbit and thus includes both the outer planets and Mars.

四颗外行星或巨行星（有时称为木星行星）合计占已知绕太阳运行质量的 99%。 木星和土星的质量是地球的 400 多倍，并且绝大多数由氢气和氦气组成，因此它们被称为气态巨行星。 天王星和海王星的质量要小得多——每个都不到地球质量的 20 个——并且主要由冰组成。 由于这些原因，一些天文学家认为它们属于自己的类别，即冰巨星。 所有四颗巨行星都有光环，但从地球上只能轻松观察到土星的光环系统。 高级行星一词指的是地球轨道之外的行星，因此包括外行星和火星。

Jupiter

Jupiter is 2.5 times the mass of all the other planets put together. It is composed largely of hydrogen and helium. Jupiter’s strong internal heat creates semi-permanent features in its atmosphere, such as cloud bands and the Great Red Spot. Jupiter has 79 known satellites. The four largest, Ganymede, Callisto, Io, and Europa, show similarities to the terrestrial planets, such as volcanism and internal heating. Ganymede, the largest satellite in the Solar System, is larger than Mercury.

木星的质量是所有其他行星质量总和的 2.5 倍。 它主要由氢和氦组成。 木星强大的内部热量在其大气层中产生了半永久性特征，例如云带和大红斑。 木星有 79 颗已知卫星。 最大的四颗行星，木卫三、木卫四、木卫一和欧罗巴，显示出与类地行星的相似之处，例如火山活动和内部加热。 木卫三是太阳系中最大的卫星，比水星还大。

Saturn

Saturn, distinguished by its extensive ring system, has several similarities to Jupiter, such as its atmospheric composition and magnetosphere. Although Saturn has 60% of Jupiter’s volume, it is less than a third as massive. Saturn is the only planet of the Solar System that is less dense than water. The rings of Saturn are made up of small ice and rock particles. Saturn has 82 confirmed satellites composed largely of ice. Two of these, Titan and Enceladus, show signs of geological activity. Titan, the second-largest moon in the Solar System, is larger than Mercury and the only satellite in the Solar System with a substantial atmosphere.

土星以其广泛的环系统而著称，与木星有一些相似之处，例如其大气成分和磁层。 尽管土星的体积是木星的 60%，但它的质量还不到木星的三分之一。 土星是太阳系中唯一一颗密度低于水的行星。 土星环由小冰和岩石颗粒组成。 土星有 82 颗已确认的主要由冰组成的卫星。 其中两个，土卫六和土卫二，显示出地质活动的迹象。 土卫六是太阳系中第二大卫星，比水星大，是太阳系中唯一一颗拥有大量大气层的卫星。

Uranus

Uranus, is the lightest of the outer planets. Uniquely among the planets, it orbits the Sun on its side; its axial tilt is over ninety degrees to the ecliptic. It has a much colder core than the other giant planets and radiates very little heat into space. Uranus has 27 known satellites, the largest ones being Titania, Oberon, Umbriel, Ariel, and Miranda.

天王星，是最轻的外行星。 在行星中独一无二的是，它绕着太阳公转； 它的轴向倾斜度超过黄道九十度。 它的核心比其他巨行星冷得多，向太空辐射的热量非常少。 天王星有 27 颗已知卫星，最大的是 Titania、Oberon、Umbriel、Ariel 和 Miranda。

Neptune

Neptune, though slightly smaller than Uranus, is more massive and hence more dense. It radiates more internal heat, but not as much as Jupiter or Saturn. Neptune has 14 known satellites. The largest, Triton, is geologically active, with geysers of liquid nitrogen. Triton is the only large satellite with a retrograde orbit. Neptune is accompanied in its orbit by several minor planets, termed Neptune trojans, that are in 1:1 resonance with it.

海王星虽然比天王星略小，但质量更大，因此密度更大。 它散发出更多的内部热量，但没有木星或土星那么多。 海王星有 14 颗已知卫星。 最大的 Triton 地质活动活跃，有液氮间歇泉。 Triton 是唯一一颗具有逆行轨道的大型卫星。 海王星在其轨道上伴随着几颗小行星，这些小行星被称为海王星特洛伊木马，它们与海王星以 1:1 共振。

工业革命

背景介绍

Overview：

The Industrial Revolution was the transition to new manufacturing processes in Europe and the United States, in the period from about 1760 to sometime between 1820 and 1840. This transition included going from hand production methods to machines, new chemical manufacturing and iron production processes, the increasing use of steam power and water power, the development of machine tools and the rise of the mechanized factory system. The Industrial Revolution also led to an unprecedented rise in the rate of population growth.

工业革命是从大约 1760 年到 1820 年至 1840 年期间欧洲和美国向新制造工艺的过渡。这种过渡包括从手工生产方法到机器、新的化学制造和铁生产工艺， 蒸汽动力和水力的使用增加，机床的发展和机械化工厂系统的兴起。 工业革命还导致人口增长率空前上升。

Textiles were the dominant industry of the Industrial Revolution in terms of employment, value of output and capital invested. The textile industry was also the first to use modern production methods.

就就业、产值和投资资本而言，纺织业是工业革命的主导产业。 纺织业也是最早使用现代生产方法的行业。

The Industrial Revolution marks a major turning point in history; almost every aspect of daily life was influenced in some way. In particular, average income and population began to exhibit unprecedented sustained growth. Some economists have said the most important effect of the Industrial Revolution was that the standard of living for the general population in the western world began to increase consistently for the first time in history, although others have said that it did not begin to meaningfully improve until the late 19th and 20th centuries.

工业革命标志着历史的重大转折点。 几乎日常生活的方方面面都受到了某种程度的影响。 特别是平均收入和人口开始出现前所未有的持续增长。 一些经济学家表示，工业革命最重要的影响是西方世界普通民众的生活水平在历史上首次开始持续提高，尽管其他人表示，工业革命直到 19 世纪末和 20 世纪。

Requirement

Six factors facilitated industrialization:

六大因素促进产业化：

  1. high levels of agricultural productivity to provide excess manpower and food;

  2. a pool of managerial and entrepreneurial skills;

  3. available ports, rivers, canals and roads to cheaply move raw materials and outputs;

  4. natural resources such as coal, iron and waterfalls;

  5. political stability and a legal system that supported business;

  6. financial capital available to invest.

    1. 高水平的农业生产力，以提供多余的人力和食物；

    2. 管理和创业技能库；

    3. 可用于廉价运输原材料和产出的港口、河流、运河和道路；

    4. 煤炭、铁和瀑布等自然资源；

    5. 政治稳定和支持商业的法律制度；

    6. 可用于投资的金融资本。

Once industrialization began in Great Britain, new factors can be added: the eagerness British entrepreneurs to export industrial expertise and the willingness to import the process. Britain met the criteria and industrialized starting in the 18th century. Britain exported the process to western Europe (especially Belgium, France and the German states) in the early 19th century. The United States copied the British model in the early 19th century and Japan copied the Western European models in the late 19th century.

一旦英国开始工业化，就可以添加新的因素：英国企业家出口工业专业知识的渴望和进口该过程的意愿。 英国符合标准并从 18 世纪开始工业化。 19 世纪初，英国将该工艺出口到西欧（尤其是比利时、法国和德国）。 美国在19世纪初复制了英国模式，日本在19世纪末复制了西欧模式。

Important technological development

The commencement of the Industrial Revolution is closely linked to a small number of innovations, beginning in the second half of the 18th century. By the 1830s the following gains had been made in important technologies:

工业革命的开始与从 18 世纪下半叶开始的少数创新密切相关。 到 1830 年代，重要技术取得了以下进展：

1. Textiles – mechanised cotton spinning powered by steam or water increased the output of a worker by a factor of around 500. The power loom increased the output of a worker by a factor of over 40. The cotton gin increased productivity of removing seed from cotton by a factor of 50. Large gains in productivity also occurred in spinning and weaving of wool and linen, but they were not as great as in cotton.

  1. 纺织——以蒸汽或水为动力的机械化棉纺使工人的产量提高了约 500 倍。动力织机使工人的产量提高了 40 多倍。轧棉机提高了从中提取种子的生产率 棉花增长了 50 倍。羊毛和亚麻的纺纱和织造生产率也有很大提高，但不如棉花那么大。

2. Steam power – the efficiency of steam engines increased so that they used between one-fifth and one-tenth as much fuel. The adaptation of stationary steam engines to rotary motion made them suitable for industrial uses. The high pressure engine had a high power to weight ratio, making it suitable for transportation. Steam power underwent a rapid expansion after 1800.

  1. 蒸汽动力——蒸汽机的效率提高了，所以它们使用的燃料只有原来的五分之一到十分之一。 固定式蒸汽机适应旋转运动使其适合工业用途。 高压发动机具有高功率重量比，适合运输。 蒸汽动力在 1800 年后经历了快速扩张。

3. Iron making – the substitution of coke for charcoal greatly lowered the fuel cost of pig iron and wrought iron production. Using coke also allowed larger blast furnaces, resulting in economies of scale. The steam engine began being used to pump water and to power blast air in the mid 1750s, enabling a large increase in iron production by overcoming the limitation of water power. The cast iron blowing cylinder was first used in 1760. It was later improved by making it double acting, which allowed higher blast furnace temperatures. The puddling process produced a structural grade iron at a lower cost than the finery forge. The rolling mill was fifteen times faster than hammering wrought iron. Hot blast (1828) greatly increased fuel efficiency in iron production in the following decades.

  1. 炼铁——焦炭替代木炭大大降低了生铁和熟铁生产的燃料成本。 使用焦炭还允许更大的高炉，从而产生规模经济。 蒸汽机在 1750 年代中期开始用于抽水和为鼓风提供动力，通过克服水力的限制使铁产量大幅增加。 铸铁鼓风缸于 1760 年首次使用。后来改进为双作用，允许更高的高炉温度。 炼钢工艺生产结构级铁的成本低于精细锻造。 轧机比锤打锻铁快十五倍。 在接下来的几十年里，热风技术（1828 年）极大地提高了炼铁生产的燃料效率。

4. Invention of machine tools – The first machine tools were invented. These included the screw cutting lathe, cylinder boring machine and the milling machine. Machine tools made the economical manufacture of precision metal parts possible, although it took several decades to develop effective techniques.

  1. 机床的发明——发明了第一台机床。 其中包括螺纹切削车床、圆筒镗床和铣床。 机床使精密金属零件的经济制造成为可能，尽管开发有效的技术需要几十年的时间。

农业起源

First Agricultural Revolution 也可以称之为Neolithic Revolution

农业起源必定意味着——–人类生活方式的变化，人类健康的变化，社会结构的变化和人口的增多。托福的考试一般不会考这么广，一般只会考其中的一个地点和部分。

Overview：Neolithic Revolution

  • Hunting and gathering to agriculture and settlement

The Neolithic Revolution, or the (First) Agricultural Revolution, was the wide-scale transition of many human cultures during the Neolithic period from a lifestyle of hunting and gathering to one of agriculture and settlement, making an increasingly large population possible. These settled communities permitted humans to observe and experiment with plants to learn how they grew and developed. This new knowledge led to the domestication of plants.

新石器时代革命，或（第一次）农业革命，是新石器时代许多人类文化从狩猎和采集生活方式向农业和定居生活方式的大规模转变，这使得越来越多的人口成为可能。 这些定居的社区允许人类观察和试验植物，以了解它们是如何生长和发育的。 这种新知识导致了植物的驯化。

  • Happaned in separate location worldwide

Archaeological data indicates that the domestication of various types of plants and animals happened in separate locations worldwide, starting in the geological epoch of the Holocene 11,700 years ago. It was the world’s first historically verifiable revolution in agriculture. The Neolithic Revolution greatly narrowed the diversity of foods available, resulting in a downturn in the quality of human nutrition.

考古资料表明，从 11700 年前的全新世地质时代开始，各种动植物的驯化发生在世界各地的不同地点。 这是世界上第一次历史可验证的农业革命。 新石器时代革命极大地缩小了可用食物的多样性，导致人类营养质量下降。

  • Food-crop cultivation

The Neolithic Revolution involved far more than the adoption of a limited set of food-producing techniques. During the next millennia it transformed the small and mobile groups of hunter-gatherers that had hitherto dominated human pre-history into sedentary (non-nomadic) societies based in built-up villages and towns. These societies radically modified their natural environment by means of specialized food-crop cultivation, with activities such as irrigation and deforestation which allowed the production of surplus food.

新石器时代革命涉及的远不止采用一套有限的食品生产技术。 在接下来的几千年里，它把迄今为止主导人类史前时期的小型、流动的狩猎采集者群体转变为以建成的村庄和城镇为基础的定居（非游牧）社会。 这些社会通过专门的粮食作物种植彻底改变了他们的自然环境，包括灌溉和砍伐森林等活动，从而生产出剩余的食物。

  • Domestication of animals

Other developments that are found very widely during this era are the deomestication of animals, pottery, polished stone tools, and rectangular houses. In many regions, the adoption of agriculture by prehistoric societies caused episodes of rapid population growth, a phenomenon known as the Neolithic demographic transition.

在这个时代广泛发现的其他发展是动物、陶器、抛光石器和矩形房屋的驯化。 在许多地区，史前社会对农业的采用导致了人口的快速增长，这种现象被称为新石器时代的人口转变。

Two important lifestyle

  1. Nomadism lifestyle

Hunter-gatherers had different subsistence requirements and lifestyles from agriculturalists. They resided in temporary shelters and were highly mobile, moving in small groups and had limited contact with outsiders. Their diet was well-balanced and depended on what the environment provided each season.

狩猎采集者与农业者有着不同的生存要求和生活方式。 他们住在临时避难所，流动性强，成群结队，与外界接触有限。 他们的饮食很均衡，取决于每个季节所提供的环境。

  1. Settlement lifestyle

Because the advent of agriculture made it possible to support larger groups, agriculturalists lived in more permanent dwellings in areas that were more densely populated than could be supported by the hunter-gatherer lifestyle. The development of trading networks and complex societies brought them into contact with outside groups.

由于农业的出现使养活更大的群体成为可能，因此农学家住在人口稠密地区的永久住所中，而狩猎采集生活方式无法支持这些地区。 贸易网络和复杂社会的发展使他们与外部团体接触。

几个重要的问题：

  • Why agriculturalists are not health?

However, population increase did not necessarily correlate with improved health. Reliance on a single crop can adversely affect health even while making it possible to support larger numbers of people. Maize is deficient in certain essential amino acids (lysine and tryptophan) and is a poor source of iron. The phytic acid it contains may inhibit nutrient absorption. Other factors that likely affected the health of early agriculturalists and their domesticated livestock would have been increased numbers of parasites and disease-bearing pests associated with human waste and contaminated food and water supplies. Fertilizers and irrigation may have increased crop yields but also would have promoted proliferation of insects and bacteria in the local environment while grain storage attracted additional insects and rodents.

然而，人口增加并不一定与健康状况改善相关。 对单一作物的依赖可能会对健康产生不利影响，即使这样可以养活更多的人。 玉米缺乏某些必需氨基酸（赖氨酸和色氨酸），并且是铁的不良来源。 它所含的植酸可能会抑制营养吸收。 其他可能影响早期农学家及其家养牲畜健康的因素包括与人类排泄物以及受污染的食物和水供应相关的寄生虫和携带疾病的害虫数量增加。 施肥和灌溉可能会增加作物产量，但也会促进当地环境中昆虫和细菌的繁殖，而粮食储存会吸引更多的昆虫和啮齿动物。

  • What caused the Agricultural revolution?

The Oasis Theory

The Oasis Theory originally proposed by Raphael Pumpelly in 1908, popularized by V. Gordon Childe in 1928 and summarised in Childe’s book Man Makes Himself. This theory maintains that as the climate got drier due to the Atlantic depressions shifting northward, communities contracted to oases where they were forced into close association with animals, which were then domesticated together with planting of seeds. However, today this theory has little support amongst archaeologists because subsequent climate data suggests that the region was getting wetter rather than drier.

绿洲理论最初由 Raphael Pumpelly 于 1908 年提出，1928 年由 V. Gordon Childe 推广并总结于 Childe 的著作 Man Makes Himself。 该理论认为，由于大西洋洼地向北移动导致气候变得干燥，社区承包到绿洲，在那里他们被迫与动物密切联系，然后在种植种子的同时被驯化。 然而，今天这一理论在考古学家中几乎没有得到支持，因为随后的气候数据表明该地区变得更湿润而不是更干燥。

The Stable Climate Theory

Peter Richerson, Robert Boyd, and Robert Bettinger make a case for the development of agriculture coinciding with an increasingly stable climate at the beginning of the Holocene. Ronald Wright’s book and Massey Lecture Series A Short History of Progress popularized this hypothesis.

彼得·里森 (Peter Richerson)、罗伯特·博伊德 (Robert Boyd) 和罗伯特·贝廷格 (Robert Bettinger) 提出了农业发展与全新世初期日益稳定的气候相吻合的案例。 罗纳德·赖特 (Ronald Wright) 的书和梅西系列讲座“进步简史”(A Short History of Progress) 推广了这一假设。

Megafauna extinction Theory

The postulated Younger Dryas impact event, claimed to be in part responsible for megafauna extinction and ending the last glacial period, could have provided circumstances that required the evolution of agricultural societies for humanity to survive.

假设的新仙女木撞击事件声称对巨型动物的灭绝和末次冰期的结束负有部分责任，可能提供了人类生存所需的农业社会进化的环境。

The Demographic Theories

The Demographic theories proposed by Carl Sauer and adapted by Lewis Binford and Kent Flannery posit an increasingly sedentary population that expanded up to the carrying capacity of the local environment and required more food than could be gathered. Various social and economic factors helped drive the need for food.

卡尔绍尔提出并由刘易斯宾福德和肯特弗兰纳里改编的人口学理论假设越来越多的久坐不动的人口扩大到当地环境的承载能力并且需要比收集更多的食物。 各种社会和经济因素帮助推动了对食物的需求。

地质学

地球构造

Overview

The internal structure of Earth, structure of the solid Earth, or simply structure of Earth refers to concentric spherical layers subdividing the Solid earth, i.e., excluding Earth’s atmosphere and hydrosphere. It consists of an outer silicate solid crust, a highly viscous asthenosphere and solid mantle, a liquid outer core whose flow generates the Earth’s magnetic field, and a solid inner core.

地球的内部结构，固体地球的结构，或简单的地球结构是指将固体地球细分的同心球层，即不包括地球大气层和水圈。 它由外部硅酸盐固体地壳、高粘性软流圈和固体地幔、流动产生地球磁场的液体外核和固体内核组成。

Scientific understanding of the internal structure of Earth is based on observations of topography and bathymetry, observations of rock in outcrop, samples brought to the surface from greater depths by volcanoes or volcanic activity, analysis of the seismic waves that pass through Earth, measurements of the gravitational and magnetic fields of Earth, and experiments with crystalline solids at pressures and temperatures characteristic of Earth’s deep interior.

对地球内部结构的科学理解基于对地形和测深的观察、对露头岩石的观察、由火山或火山活动从更深处带回地表的样本、对穿过地球的地震波的分析、对地震波的测量 地球的引力和磁场，以及在地球深层内部的压力和温度特征下对结晶固体进行的实验。

  • Two ways to define the structure of Earth

The structure of Earth can be defined in two ways: by mechanical properties such as rheology, or chemically.

地球的结构可以通过两种方式来定义：通过机械特性（例如流变学）或化学特性。

1. Mechanically, it can be divided into lithosphere, asthenosphere, mesospheric mantle, outer core, and the inner core.

  1. 力学上可分为岩石圈、软流圈、中层地幔、外核、内核。

2. Chemically, Earth can be divided into the crust, upper mantle, lower mantle, outer core, and inner core.

  1. 在化学上，地球可分为地壳、上地幔、下地幔、外核和内核。
  • How to inferred that earth has different layers？

The layering of Earth has been inferred indirectly using the time of travel of refracted and reflected seismic waves created by earthquakes. The core does not allow shear waves to pass through it, while the speed of travel (seismic velocity) is different in other layers. The changes in seismic velocity between different layers causes refraction owing to Snell’s law, like light bending as it passes through a prism. Likewise, reflections are caused by a large increase in seismic velocity and are similar to light reflecting from a mirror.

地球的分层是利用地震产生的折射和反射地震波的传播时间间接推断出来的。 核心不允许剪切波通过它，而行进速度（地震速度）在其他层中是不同的。 由于斯涅尔定律，不同层之间地震速度的变化会引起折射，就像光线通过棱镜时发生弯曲一样。 同样，反射是由地震速度的大幅增加引起的，类似于从镜子反射的光

Crust

Earth’s crust is a thin shell on the outside of Earth, accounting for less than 1% of Earth’s volume. The thin parts are the oceanic crust, which underlie the ocean basins (5–10 km) and are composed of dense iron magnesium silicate igneous rocks, like basalt. The thicker crust is continental crust, which is less dense and composed of sodium potassium aluminium silicate rocks, like granite. Earth’s crust is the top component of the lithosphere, a division of Earth’s layers that includes the crust and the upper part of the mantle. The lithosphere is broken into tectonic plates whose motion allows heat to escape from the interior of the Earth into space.

地壳是地球外部的薄壳，占地球体积的不到1%。 薄的部分是海洋地壳，它位于海盆下面（5-10 公里），由致密的铁镁硅酸盐火成岩组成，如玄武岩。 较厚的地壳是大陆地壳，密度较小，由钠钾铝硅酸盐岩石组成，如花岗岩。 地壳是岩石圈的顶部组成部分，岩石圈是地球各层的一个部分，包括地壳和地幔上部。 岩石圈被分解成构造板块，这些板块的运动使热量从地球内部散逸到太空中。

The crust lies on top of the mantle, a configuration that is stable because the upper mantle is made of peridotite and so is significantly more dense than the crust. The boundary between the crust and mantle is conventionally placed at the Mohorovičić discontinuity, a boundary defined by a contrast in seismic velocity.

地壳位于地幔之上，这是一种稳定的结构，因为上地幔由橄榄岩构成，因此比地壳密度大得多。 地壳和地幔之间的边界通常位于 Mohorovičić 不连续面，该边界由地震速度的对比定义。

The temperature of the crust increases with depth, reaching values typically in the range from about 100 °C (212 °F) to 600 °C (1,112 °F) at the boundary with the underlying mantle. The temperature increases by as much as 30 °C (54 °F) for every kilometer locally in the upper part of the crust.

地壳的温度随着深度的增加而升高，在与下层地幔的边界处通常达到约 100 °C (212 °F) 至 600 °C (1,112 °F) 的值。 在地壳上部局部地区，每公里温度升高 30 °C（54 °F）。

Mantle

Earth’s mantle extends to a depth of 2,890 km, making it the planet’s thickest layer. The pressure at the bottom of the mantle is ≈140 GPa (1.4 Matm). The mantle is composed of silicate rocks richer in iron and magnesium than the overlying crust. Although solid, the mantle’s extremely hot silicate material can flow over very long timescales. Convection of the mantle propels the motion of the tectonic plates in the crust. The source of heat that drives this motion is the primordial heat left over from the planet’s formation renewed by the radioactive decay of uranium, thorium, and potassium in Earth’s crust and mantle.

地球的地幔延伸至 2,890 公里的深度，使其成为地球上最厚的地层。 地幔底部的压力≈140 GPa (1.4 Matm)。 地幔由比上覆地壳富含铁和镁的硅酸盐岩石组成。 虽然是固体，但地幔中极热的硅酸盐物质可以在很长的时间尺度内流动。 地幔的对流推动地壳中构造板块的运动。 驱动这一运动的热源是行星形成过程中遗留下来的原始热量，这些原始热量被地壳和地幔中铀、钍和钾的放射性衰变所更新。

Core

The average density of Earth is 5.515 g/cm3. Because the average density of surface material is only around 3.0 g/cm3, we must conclude that denser materials exist within Earth’s core. Seismic measurements show that the core is divided into two parts, a “solid” inner core with a radius of ≈1,220 km and a liquid outer core extending beyond it to a radius of ≈3,400 km. The densities are between 9,900 and 12,200 kg/m3 in the outer core and 12,600–13,000 kg/m3 in the inner core.

地球的平均密度为 5.515 g/cm3。 由于地表物质的平均密度仅为 3.0 g/cm3 左右，我们必须得出结论，地核内存在密度更大的物质。 地震测量表明，地核分为两部分，一个半径约为 1,220 公里的“固体”内核和一个延伸至半径约 3,400 公里的液态外核。 外核的密度在 9,900 到 12,200 kg/m3 之间，内核的密度在 12,600-13,000 kg/m3 之间。

  • Earth’s inner core

Earth’s inner core is the innermost geologic layer of the planet Earth. It is primarily a solid ball with a radius of about 1,220 km (760 mi), which is about 20% of Earth’s radius or 70% of the Moon’s radius.

地球内核是地球最内层的地质层。 它主要是一个半径约为 1,220 公里（760 英里）的实心球，约为地球半径的 20% 或月球半径的 70%。

There are no samples of Earth’s core accessible for direct measurement, as there are for Earth’s mantle. Information about Earth’s core mostly comes from analysis of seismic waves and Earth’s magnetic field. The inner core is believed to be composed of an iron–nickel alloy with some other elements. The temperature at the inner core’s surface is estimated to be approximately 5,700 K (5,430 °C; 9,800 °F), which is about the temperature at the surface of the Sun.

没有像地幔那样可用于直接测量的地核样本。 有关地核的信息主要来自对地震波和地球磁场的分析。 内核被认为是由铁镍合金和一些其他元素组成的。 内核表面的温度估计约为 5,700 K（5,430 °C；9,800 °F），这大约是太阳表面的温度。

  • Earth’s outer core

Earth’s outer core is a fluid layer about 2,400 km (1,500 mi) thick and composed of mostly iron and nickel that lies above Earth’s solid inner core and below its mantle. Its outer boundary lies 2,890 km (1,800 mi) beneath Earth’s surface. The transition between the inner core and outer core is located approximately 5,150 km (3,200 mi) beneath the Earth’s surface. Unlike the inner (or solid) core, the outer core is liquid. The magnetic field is generated by electric currents due to the motion of convection currents of a mixture of molten iron and nickel in the Earth’s outer core: these convection currents are caused by heat escaping from the core, a natural process called a geodynamo.

地球的外核是一个约 2,400 公里（1,500 英里）厚的流体层，主要由铁和镍组成，位于地球固体内核上方和地幔下方。 它的外边界位于地球表面以下 2,890 公里（1,800 英里）处。 内核和外核之间的过渡位于地球表面以下约 5,150 公里（3,200 英里）处。 与内核（或固体）不同，外核是液态的。 磁场是由地球外核中铁水和镍混合物的对流运动产生的电流产生的：这些对流是由地核逸出的热量引起的，这是一种称为地球发电机的自然过程。

Continental Drift

Overview：

Continental drift is the hypothesis that the Earth’s continents have moved over geologic time relative to each other, thus appearing to have “drifted” across the ocean bed. The speculation that continents might have ‘drifted’ was first put forward by Abraham Ortelius in 1596. The concept was independently and more fully developed by Alfred Wegener in 1912, but his hypothesis was rejected by many for lack of any motive mechanism. Arthur Holmes later proposed mantle convection for that mechanism. The idea of continental drift has since been subsumed by the theory of plate tectonics, which explains that the continents move by riding on plates of the Earth’s lithosphere.

大陆漂移是地球大陆相对于彼此在地质时间上移动的假设，因此看起来已经“漂移”过海床。 大陆可能“漂移”的推测最早是由亚伯拉罕·奥特留斯于1596年提出的。这一概念在1912年由阿尔弗雷德·韦格纳独立且更充分地发展，但他的假设因缺乏任何动力机制而被许多人拒绝。 Arthur Holmes 后来为该机制提出了地幔对流。 大陆漂移的概念后来被板块构造理论所包含，板块构造理论解释了大陆是靠地球岩石圈的板块运动的。

The continental drift hypothesis was developed in the early part of the 20th century, mostly by Alfred Wegener. Wegener said that continents move around on Earth’s surface and that they were once joined together as a single supercontinent. While Wegener was alive, scientists did not believe that the continents could move. Alfred Wegener proposed that the continents were once united into a single supercontinent named Pangaea. He suggested that Pangaea broke up long ago and that the continents then moved to their current positions. He called his hypothesis continental drift.

大陆漂移假说是在 20 世纪早期提出的，主要由阿尔弗雷德·魏格纳提出。 韦格纳说，大陆在地球表面四处移动，它们曾经连接在一起成为一个单一的超级大陆。 魏格纳在世时，科学家们不相信大陆会移动。 阿尔弗雷德·魏格纳 (Alfred Wegener) 提出，这些大陆曾经联合成一个名为盘古大陆的超大陆。 他建议盘古大陆很久以前就分裂了，然后大陆移动到现在的位置。 他称他的假设为大陆漂移。

Key Principles

The outer layers of the Earth are divided into the lithosphere and asthenosphere. The division is based on differences in mechanical properties and in the method for the transfer of heat. The lithosphere is cooler and more rigid, while the asthenosphere is hotter and flows more easily. This division should not be confused with the chemical subdivision of these same layers into the mantle (comprising both the asthenosphere and the mantle portion of the lithosphere) and the crust: a given piece of mantle may be part of the lithosphere or the asthenosphere at different times depending on its temperature and pressure.

地球的外层分为岩石圈和软流圈。 这种划分是基于机械性能和热传递方法的差异。 岩石圈较冷且更坚硬，而软流圈较热且更容易流动。 这种划分不应与这些相同层的化学细分混淆为地幔（包括软流圈和岩石圈的地幔部分）和地壳：给定的一块地幔可能是岩石圈或软流圈的一部分在不同的 时间取决于它的温度和压力。

The key principle of plate tectonics is that the lithosphere exists as separate and distinct tectonic plates, which ride on the fluid-like asthenosphere. Plate motions range up to a typical 10–40 mm/year (Mid-Atlantic Ridge; about as fast as fingernails grow), to about 160 mm/year (Nazca Plate; about as fast as hair grows). 板块构造学的关键原理是，岩石圈作为独立且不同的构造板块存在，它们位于类似流体的软流圈上。 板块运动的范围通常高达 10-40 毫米/年（大西洋中脊；大约与指甲生长一样快）到大约 160 毫米/年（纳斯卡板块；大约与头发生长一样快）。 该运动背后的驱动机制描述如下。

Evidence

1. Ancient fossils 化石

Ancient fossils of the same species of extinct plants and animals are found in rocks of the same age but are on continents that are now widely separated. Wegener proposed that the organisms had lived side by side, but that the lands had moved apart after they were dead and fossilized. He suggested that the organisms would not have been able to travel across the oceans. For example, the fossils of the seed fern Glossopteris were too heavy to be carried so far by wind. The reptile Mesosaurus was a swimming reptile but could only swim in fresh water. Cynognathus and Lystrosaurus were land reptiles and were unable to swim.

在同一时代的岩石中发现了同种已灭绝动植物的古代化石，但它们分布在如今相距甚远的大陆上。 魏格纳提出，这些生物曾经并肩生活，但在它们死去并变成化石后，陆地就分开了。 他认为这些生物不可能穿越海洋。 例如，种子蕨类植物 Glossopteris 的化石太重，无法被风吹到那么远的地方。 爬行动物中龙是一种游泳爬行动物，但只能在淡水中游泳。 Cynognathus 和 Lystrosaurus 是陆地爬行动物，不会游泳。

2. Same rock types in Appalachians and Caledonides 山脉

Besides the way the continents fit together, Wegener and his supporters collected a great deal of evidence for the continental drift hypothesis. For one, identical rocks of the same type and age are found on both sides of the Atlantic Ocean. Wegener said the rocks had formed side-by-side and that the land had since moved apart. Mountain ranges with the same rock types, structures, and ages are now on opposite sides of the Atlantic Ocean. The Appalachians of the eastern United States and Canada, for example, are just like mountain (Caledonides) ranges in eastern Greenland, Ireland, Great Britain, and Norway. Wegener concluded that they formed as a single mountain range that was separated as the continents drifted.

除了大陆拼合的方式外，魏格纳和他的支持者还为大陆漂移假说收集了大量证据。 一方面，在大西洋两岸发现了相同类型和年龄的相同岩石。 韦格纳说，这些岩石是并排形成的，而这片土地已经分开了。 具有相同岩石类型、结构和年龄的山脉现在位于大西洋的两侧。 例如，美国东部和加拿大的阿巴拉契亚山脉就像格陵兰岛东部、爱尔兰、英国和挪威的山脉（喀里多尼德山脉）。 魏格纳得出结论，它们形成了一个单一的山脉，随着大陆的漂移而分开。

3. Grooves and rock deposits found on different continents 冰川

Grooves and rock deposits left by ancient glaciers are found today on different continents very close to the equator. This would indicate that the glaciers either formed in the middle of the ocean and/or covered most of the Earth. Today glaciers only form on land of higher elevation and nearer the poles. Wegener thought that the glaciers were centered over the southern land mass close to the South Pole and the continents moved to their present positions later on.

今天在非常靠近赤道的不同大陆上发现了古代冰川留下的沟槽和岩石沉积物。 这表明冰川要么形成于海洋中部，要么覆盖了地球的大部分地区。 今天，冰川只在海拔较高且靠近两极的土地上形成。 魏格纳认为冰川集中在靠近南极的南部陆地，后来大陆移动到现在的位置。

4. Coal found in cold region 珊瑚

Coral reefs and coal-forming swamps are found in tropical and subtropical environments, but ancient coal seams and coral reefs are found in locations where it is much too cold today, such as the Arctic Pole and the North Coastline of Russia. Wegener suggested that these creatures were alive in warm climate zones and that the fossils and coal later had drifted to new locations on the continents.

珊瑚礁和煤形成沼泽存在于热带和亚热带环境中，但古老的煤层和珊瑚礁存在于如今天气太冷的地方，例如北极和俄罗斯北部海岸线。 魏格纳认为这些生物生活在温暖的气候区，化石和煤炭后来漂流到大陆上的新地点。

Weathering and Erosion

说白了区别就是 weathering - 不动 ， erosion - 动。

While weathering and erosion are similar processes, they are not synonymous. Weathering involves the breakdown of rocks and minerals on Earth, whereas erosion involves the removal of soil and rock materials.

虽然风化和侵蚀是相似的过程，但它们并不是同义词。 风化涉及地球上岩石和矿物的分解，而侵蚀涉及土壤和岩石材料的去除

Weathering

Weathering is the breaking down of rocks, soils, and minerals as well as wood and artificial materials through contact with water, atmospheric gases, and biological organisms. Weathering occurs in situ (on site, with little or no movement), and should not be confused with erosion, which involves the transport of rocks and minerals by agents such as water, ice, snow, wind, waves and gravity.

风化是岩石、土壤和矿物以及木材和人造材料通过与水、大气和生物有机体接触而分解。 风化发生在原地（现场，很少或根本没有移动），不应与侵蚀相混淆，侵蚀涉及通过水、冰、雪、风、波浪和重力等因素运输岩石和矿物。

While physical weathering is most rapid in very cold or very dry environments, chemical reactions are most rapid where the climate is wet and hot. However, both types of weathering occur together, and each tends to accelerate the other. For example, frost weathering creates cracks in the surface of a rocky outcrop, making it more susceptible to chemical reactions by providing paths for water and air to penetrate the rock.

物理风化在非常寒冷或非常干燥的环境中最快，而化学反应在气候潮湿和炎热的地方最快。 然而，这两种类型的风化作用同时发生，并且每一种都倾向于加速另一种。 例如，霜冻风化会在岩石露头表面产生裂缝，为水和空气提供穿透岩石的路径，使其更容易发生化学反应。

Types of weathering

There are two basic types of weathering, mechanical weathering and chemical weathering.

风化有两种基本类型，机械风化和化学风化。

  • Mechanical weathering (physical weathering) makes rocks crumble.

Physical weathering involves the breakdown of rocks and soils through the mechanical effects of heat, water, ice, or other agents.

物理风化涉及通过热、水、冰或其他介质的机械作用使岩石和土壤分解。

Example:

  • Ice/Frost Wedging

Many rock surfaces have small cracks on them. Water can freeze in these cracks when it is cold, and then melt when the weather is warmer. This repeated freezing and thawing creates ice wedges, which can cause rocks to break**.**

许多岩石表面都有小裂缝。 水在寒冷时会在这些裂缝中结冰，然后在天气变暖时融化。 这种反复的冻结和解冻会产生冰楔，从而导致岩石破裂。

Roots of plants growing into cracks may put pressure on the surrounding rock, eventually breaking rocks apart as the roots grow.

植物的根长入裂缝可能会对周围的岩石施加压力，最终随着根的生长将岩石分开。

  • ** Chemical weathering** changes rock or soil molecular structure.

Chemical weathering involves the chemical reaction of water, atmospheric gases, and biologically produced chemicals with rocks and soils.

化学风化涉及水、大气气体和生物产生的化学物质与岩石和土壤的化学反应。

Example:

  • Acidification

You’ve probably heard of acid rain. Acid rain is water with sulfuric and nitric acids from the burning of coal and fossil fuels, along with volcano eruptions. The acids create a reaction when they hit stone, causing the surface to wear and the composition to soften. Acidification can also be caused by organisms like lichens, which are created from algae and fungi.

Within the weathering environment, chemical oxidation of a variety of metals occurs. 在风化环境中，多种金属发生化学氧化。 最常见的是 Fe2+（铁）被氧气和水氧化形成 Fe3+ 氧化物和氢氧化物，如针铁矿、褐铁矿和赤铁矿。 这使得受影响的岩石表面呈红棕色，很容易破碎并削弱岩石。 许多其他金属矿石和矿物会氧化和水合以产生有色沉积物，就像硫化物矿物（如黄铜矿或 CuFeS2 氧化成氢氧化铜和氧化铁）风化过程中的硫一样。

Erosion

In arth science, erosion is the action of surface processes (such as water flow or wind) that removes soil, rock, or dissolved material from one location on the Earth’s crust, and then transports it to another location. Erosion is distinct from weathering which involves no movement.

在地球科学中，侵蚀是地表过程（例如水流或风）的作用，将土壤、岩石或溶解物质从地壳的一个位置移除，然后将其运送到另一个位置。 侵蚀不同于不涉及运动的风化。

Removal of rock or soil as clastic sediment is referred to as physical or mechanical erosion; this contrasts with chemical erosion, where soil or rock material is removed from an area by dissolution.

将岩石或土壤作为碎屑沉积物去除称为物理或机械侵蚀； 这与化学侵蚀形成对比，在化学侵蚀中，土壤或岩石材料通过溶解从一个区域移除。

Types of Erosion

If you look at examples of erosion, you’ll see that there aren’t necessarily types of erosion, but different methods of erosion.

如果你看侵蚀的例子，你会发现不一定有侵蚀的类型，而是不同的侵蚀方法。

  • Splash Erosion

This type of erosion is caused by rainfall. It is the first stage in the process of water erosion. When raindrops hit the soil, they cause individual soil particles on the ground to disperse and splash back down. This makes it difficult for rain to permeate the ground, so the water builds up on the surface and leads to the formation of moving water, in the form of runoff.

这种类型的侵蚀是由降雨引起的。 它是水蚀过程的第一阶段。 当雨滴落在土壤上时，它们会导致地面上的单个土壤颗粒分散并飞溅回来。 这使得雨水难以渗入地面，因此水会积聚在地表并导致形成流动的水，以径流的形式出现。

Mass movement is the downward and outward movement of rock and sediments on a sloped surface, mainly due to the force of gravity.

质量运动是岩石和沉积物在倾斜表面上的向下和向外运动，主要是由于重力。

Mass movement is an important part of the erosional process and is often the first stage in the breakdown and transport of weathered materials in mountainous areas. It moves material from higher elevations to lower elevations where other eroding agents such as streams and glaciers can then pick up the material and move it to even lower elevations. Mass-movement processes are always occurring continuously on all slopes; some mass-movement processes act very slowly; others occur very suddenly, often with disastrous results.

质量运动是侵蚀过程的重要组成部分，通常是山区风化物质分解和运输的第一阶段。 它将物质从较高海拔移动到较低海拔，然后其他侵蚀剂（例如溪流和冰川）可以吸收这些物质并将其移动到更低的海拔。 质量运动过程总是在所有斜坡上连续发生； 一些群众运动过程非常缓慢； 另一些则发生得非常突然，往往会带来灾难性的后果。

Slumping happens on steep hillsides, occurring along distinct fracture zones, often within materials like clay that, once released, may move quite rapidly downhill. In some cases, the slump is caused by water beneath the slope weakening it.

坍塌发生在陡峭的山坡上，沿着明显的断裂带发生，通常在粘土等材料中，一旦释放，可能会迅速下坡。 在某些情况下，塌陷是由斜坡下方的水削弱斜坡造成的。

Water Cycle

Overview

The water cycle, also known as the hydrologic cycle or the hydrological cycle, describes the continuous movement of water on, above and below the surface of the Earth. The mass of water on Earth remains fairly constant over time but the partitioning of the water into the major reservoirs of ice, fresh water, saline water and atmospheric water is variable depending on a wide range of climatic variables. The water moves from one reservoir to another, such as from river to ocean, or from the ocean to the atmosphere, by the physical processes of evaporation, condensation, precipitation, infiltration, surface runoff, and subsurface flow. In doing so, the water goes through different forms: liquid, solid (ice) and vapor.

水循环，也称为水文循环或水文循环，描述了水在地球表面、上方和下方的连续运动。 地球上的水量随着时间的推移保持相当恒定，但水在冰、淡水、盐水和大气水等主要水库中的分配是可变的，具体取决于广泛的气候变量。 通过蒸发、凝结、降水、渗透、地表径流和地下水流等物理过程，水从一个水库流向另一个水库，例如从河流流向海洋，或从海洋流向大气。 在这样做的过程中，水经历不同的形式：液体、固体（冰）和蒸汽。

  • The importance of water cycle

The water cycle involves the exchange of energy, which leads to temperature changes. When water evaporates, it takes up energy from its surroundings and cools the environment. When it condenses, it releases energy and warms the environment. These heat exchanges influence climate.

水循环涉及能量交换，从而导致温度变化。 当水蒸发时，它会从周围吸收能量并使环境降温。 当它凝结时，它会释放能量并使环境变暖。 这些热交换影响气候。

The evaporative phase of the cycle purifies water which then replenishes the land with freshwater. The flow of liquid water and ice transports minerals across the globe. It is also involved in reshaping the geological features of the Earth, through processes including erosion and sedimentation. The water cycle is also essential for the maintenance of most life and ecosystems on the planet.

循环的蒸发阶段净化水，然后用淡水补充土地。 液态水和冰的流动将矿物质输送到全球各地。 它还通过包括侵蚀和沉积在内的过程参与重塑地球的地质特征。 水循环对于地球上大多数生命和生态系统的维持也是必不可少的。

  • The principle of the water cycle

The sun, which drives the water cycle, heats water in oceans and seas. Water evaporates as water vapor into the air. Some ice and snow sublimates directly into water vapor. Evapotranspiration is water transpired from plants and evaporated from the soil. The water molecule H2O has smaller molecular mass than the major components of the atmosphere, nitrogen and oxygen, N2 and O2, hence is less dense. Due to the significant difference in density, buoyancy drives humid air higher. As altitude increases, air pressure decreases and the temperature drops. The lower temperature causes water vapor to condense into tiny liquid water droplets which are heavier than the air, and fall unless supported by an updraft. A huge concentration of these droplets over a large space up in the atmosphere become visible as cloud. Some condensation is near ground level, and called fog.

驱动水循环的太阳加热海洋中的水。 水以水蒸气的形式蒸发到空气中。 有些冰雪直接升华成水蒸气。 蒸发蒸腾是从植物中蒸发并从土壤中蒸发的水分。 水分子H2O的分子质量小于大气的主要成分氮气和氧气N2和O2，因此密度较低。 由于密度的显着差异，浮力将潮湿的空气推高。 随着高度的增加，气压降低，温度下降。 较低的温度导致水蒸气凝结成比空气重的微小液态水滴，除非有上升气流的支持，否则就会下落。 这些液滴大量集中在大气中的一个大空间上，变成可见的云。 一些凝结物靠近地面，称为雾。

Atmospheric circulation moves water vapor around the globe; cloud particles collide, grow, and fall out of the upper atmospheric layers as precipitation. Some precipitation falls as snow or hail, sleet, and can accumulate as ice caps and glaciers, which can store frozen water for thousands of years. Most water falls back into the oceans or onto land as rain, where the water flows over the ground as surface runoff. A portion of runoff enters rivers in valleys in the landscape, with streamflow moving water towards the oceans. Runoff and water emerging from the ground (groundwater) may be stored as freshwater in lakes. Not all runoff flows into rivers; much of it soaks into the ground as infiltration. Some water infiltrates deep into the ground and replenishes aquifers, which can store freshwater for long periods of time. Some infiltration stays close to the land surface and can seep back into surface-water bodies (and the ocean) as groundwater discharge. Some groundwater finds openings in the land surface and comes out as freshwater springs. In river valleys and floodplains, there is often continuous water exchange between surface water and ground water in the hyporheic zone. Over time, the water returns to the ocean, to continue the water cycle.

大气环流在全球范围内移动水蒸气； 云粒子碰撞、增长，并作为降水从大气层上层落下。 一些降水以雪或冰雹、雨夹雪的形式落下，并可以积累成冰盖和冰川，可以将冻结的水储存数千年。 大多数水以雨水的形式回落到海洋或陆地上，然后水以地表径流的形式流过地面。 一部分径流进入景观中山谷中的河流，溪流将水流向海洋。 从地下涌出的径流和水（地下水）可以作为淡水储存在湖泊中。 并非所有径流都流入河流； 其中大部分渗入地下作为渗透。 一些水渗入地下深处，补充含水层，可以长期储存淡水。 一些渗透靠近陆地表面，并可以作为地下水排放渗回地表水体（和海洋）。 一些地下水在地表找到开口并作为淡水泉涌出。 在河谷和漫滩中，潜流带的地表水和地下水之间常有连续的水交换。 随着时间的推移，水又回到海洋，继续水循环。

Urban Heat Islan

Overview

An urban heat island (UHI) is an urban area or metropolitan area that is significantly warmer than its surrounding rural areas due to human activities. The temperature difference is usually larger at night than during the day, and is most apparent when winds are weak. UHI is most noticeable during the summer and winter. The main cause of the urban heat island effect is from the modification of land surfaces. Waste heat generated by energy usage is a secondary contributor. As a population center grows, it tends to expand its area and increase its average temperature. The term heat island is also used; the term can be used to refer to any area that is relatively hotter than the surrounding, but generally refers to human-disturbed areas.

城市热岛 (UHI) 是指由于人类活动而明显高于周边农村地区的城市地区或大都市地区。 夜间的温差通常比白天大，在微风时最为明显。 UHI 在夏季和冬季最为明显。 城市热岛效应的主要原因是地表的改变。 能源使用产生的废热是次要贡献者。 随着人口中心的增长，它往往会扩大其面积并增加其平均温度。 热岛一词也被使用； 该术语可用于指代比周围相对热的任何区域，但通常指人类干扰区域。

Causes

There are several causes of an urban heat island (UHI):

1. Dark surfaces absorb significantly more solar radiation, which causes urban concentrations of roads and buildings to heat more than suburban and rural areas during the day.

  1. 深色表面会吸收更多的太阳辐射，导致城市集中的道路和建筑物在白天比郊区和农村地区受热更多。

2. Materials commonly used in urban areas for pavement and roofs, such as concrete and asphalt, have significantly different thermal bulk properties (including heat capacity and thermal conductivity) and surface radiative properties (albedo and emissivity) than the surrounding rural areas. This causes a change in the energy budget of the urban area, often leading to higher temperatures than surrounding rural areas.

  1. 城市地区常用的路面和屋顶材料，如混凝土和沥青，与周边农村地区相比，具有显着不同的热体积特性（包括热容量和导热系数）和表面辐射特性（反照率和发射率）。 这导致城市地区的能源预算发生变化，通常导致比周边农村地区更高的温度。

3. Another major reason is the lack of evapotranspiration (for example, through lack of vegetation) in urban areas. The U.S. Forest Service found in 2018 that cities in the United States are losing 36 million trees each year. With a decreased amount of vegetation, cities also lose the shade and evaporative cooling effect of trees.

  1. 另一个主要原因是城市地区缺乏蒸发蒸腾（例如，由于缺乏植被）。 美国林务局在 2018 年发现，美国城市每年损失 3600 万棵树。 随着植被数量的减少，城市也失去了树木的遮荫和蒸发冷却作用。

4. Other causes of a UHI are due to geometric effects. The tall buildings within many urban areas provide multiple surfaces for the reflection and absorption of sunlight, increasing the efficiency with which urban areas are heated. This is called the “urban canyon effect”.

  1. UHI 的其他原因是由于几何效应。 许多城市地区的高层建筑为反射和吸收阳光提供了多个表面，从而提高了城市地区的供暖效率。 这被称为“城市峡谷效应”。

5. Another effect of buildings is the blocking of wind, which also inhibits cooling by convection and prevents pollutants from dissipating. Waste heat from automobiles, air conditioning, industry, and other sources also contributes to the UHI.

​ 5、建筑物的另一个作用是挡风，也抑制对流降温，阻止污染物散逸。 来自汽车、空调、工业和其他来源的废热也有助于 UHI。

6. High levels of pollution in urban areas can also increase the UHI, as many forms of pollution change the radiative properties of the atmosphere. UHI not only raises urban temperatures but also increases ozone concentrations because ozone is a greenhouse gas whose formation will accelerate with the increase of temperature.

  1. 城市地区的高污染程度也会增加 UHI，因为多种形式的污染会改变大气的辐射特性。 UHI 不仅会提高城市温度，还会增加臭氧浓度，因为臭氧是一种温室气体，其形成会随着温度的升高而加快。

Diurnal behavior

It is well-known that compared to non-urban areas urban heat islands raise night-time temperatures more than daytime temperatures. Besides, the air temperature difference between the UHI and the surrounding environment is large at night and small during the day.

众所周知，与非城市地区相比，城市热岛使夜间温度高于白天温度。 此外，城市热岛与周围环境的空气温差夜间大，白天小。

  • Daytime

Throughout the daytime, particularly when the skies are cloudless, urban surfaces are warmed by the absorption of solar radiation. Surfaces in the urban areas tend to warm faster than those of the surrounding rural areas. By virtue of their high heat capacities, urban surfaces act as a giant reservoir of heat energy. For example, concrete can hold roughly 2,000 times as much heat as an equivalent volume of air. As a result, the large daytime surface temperature within the UHI is easily seen via thermal remote sensing. As is often the case with daytime heating, this warming also has the effect of generating convective winds within the urban boundary layer. It is theorized that, due to the atmospheric mixing that results, the air temperature perturbation within the UHI is generally minimal or nonexistent during the day, though the surface temperatures can reach extremely high levels.

在整个白天，特别是当天空万里无云时，城市表面因吸收太阳辐射而变暖。 城市地区的地表往往比周围农村地区的地表变暖得更快。 凭借其高热容量，城市表面充当了巨大的热能储存库。 例如，混凝土可容纳的热量大约是同等体积空气的 2,000 倍。 因此，通过热遥感很容易看到 UHI 内白天较大的地表温度。 与白天供暖的情况一样，这种变暖也会在城市边界层内产生对流风。 从理论上讲，由于大气混合的结果，UHI 内的气温扰动在白天通常很小或不存在，尽管地表温度可以达到极高的水平。

  • Night

At night, the situation reverses. The absence of solar heating leads to the decrease of atmospheric convection and the stabilization of urban boundary layer. If enough stabilization occurs, an inversion layer is formed. This traps urban air near the surface, and keeping surface air warm from the still-warm urban surfaces, resulting in warmer nighttime air temperatures within the UHI. Other than the heat retention properties of urban areas, the nighttime maximum in urban canyons could also be due to the blocking of “sky view” during cooling: surfaces lose heat at night principally by radiation to the comparatively cool sky, and this is blocked by the buildings in an urban area. Radiative cooling is more dominant when wind speed is low and the sky is cloudless, and indeed the UHI is found to be largest at night in these conditions.

到了晚上，情况发生逆转。 没有太阳能加热导致大气对流减少和城市边界层稳定。 如果发生足够的稳定，就会形成反型层。 这会将城市空气困在地表附近，并使仍然温暖的城市表面的地表空气保持温暖，从而导致 UHI 内夜间气温升高。 除了城市地区的保温特性外，城市峡谷的夜间最大值也可能是由于冷却过程中“天空视野”的阻挡：表面在夜间主要通过辐射到相对凉爽的天空而失去热量，而这被 市区的建筑物。 当风速低且天空万里无云时，辐射冷却更占优势，事实上，在这些条件下，UHI 被发现在夜间最大。
